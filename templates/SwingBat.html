How to Get a Good Swing In

1: Stance: Begin with a shoulder-width stance, knees slightly bent, weight evenly distributed.

2: Grip: Hold the bat firmly but not too tight, with your dominant hand at the bottom (closer to the knob).

3: Ready Position: Keep the bat parallel to the ground at shoulder height, elbows relaxed and bent.

4: Load: Shift weight back onto your back leg, raising the bat slightly behind your rear shoulder.

5: Stride: Take a small, balanced step forward with your front foot.

6: Rotation: Initiate hip and torso rotation toward the pitcher, transferring weight to your front foot.

7: Hands to the Ball: Bring the bat through the hitting zone, aiming for middle contact.

8: Contact: Keep eyes on the ball, making contact slightly in front of the plate with head down.

9: Follow Through: Continue the swing motion fully, allowing the bat to extend and body to rotate.

10: Finish: Complete the swing with balanced weight on your front foot and body in an athletic position.
